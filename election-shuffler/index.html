+ How this works

Checked candidates move together as a group. A -10 Black population shift removes up to 10 points from their combined support in precincts with the highest Black population share; lower-share precincts move less. Selecting every candidate leaves relative support unchanged.

Support settings do not change turnout. They redistribute the displayed candidate vote within each precinct, so a +10 setting can produce a smaller districtwide change after every precinct is combined.

When a candidate has a district or past election pattern, the change can be concentrated there. Results for the full district are calculated from the precinct results.

Methods and sources

How the shuffler is built

The election result is the starting point. Census characteristics determine where a demographic or turnout change is strongest, while earlier elections can help focus a candidate's change around an established geographic base. Every district total is rebuilt from the changed precincts.

What this can show: how different assumptions change the geographic coalition behind a result.

What it cannot show: how any individual person will vote or prove that votes transferred between candidates. Precinct relationships describe places, not individual voters, and the 2026 results are unofficial until certified.
