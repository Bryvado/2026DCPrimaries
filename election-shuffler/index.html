+ How this works

Demographic sliders test stronger or weaker links between a precinct characteristic and candidate support. “Black population,” for example, is the ACS estimate of the percentage of residents identifying as Black. It does not change who lives in the precinct.

Candidate support settings do not change turnout. They raise or lower support across precincts, then each precinct is brought back to a total of 100% across all candidates. That is why a +10 setting can produce a smaller final change.

When a candidate has a district or past election pattern, the change can be concentrated there. Results for the full district are calculated from the precinct results.

How to read this page

Support controls change how votes are divided among candidates. Turnout controls change the number of votes cast in precincts associated with a demographic characteristic. District totals are always rebuilt from precinct results.
